Bottom Line for the Staff

This was a character win. Virginia fell behind 1-3 in Q1 on two free position goals, then flipped the script with a dominant Q2 (5-1) driven by ball movement and settled offense. The Q3 shutout was brutal — Notre Dame's 3-0 run tied it and took the lead — but Q4 showed growth: Galica's FP goal, Dinardo's man-up finish, and Alaimo's insurance goal were all composed, high-IQ plays. The difference from the Maryland game? Discipline improved (only 1 man-up goal allowed vs 3), and the offense showed it can respond to adversity. Galica was the best player on the field (3G-2A-8DC). The Q3 turnover problem (6 TOs, 0 goals) has to be addressed — that quarter nearly cost us the game. But the defense shutting out Notre Dame in Q4 (0 goals, 3 shots) was the defining stretch. This team is growing.
